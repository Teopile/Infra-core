# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Office managers, procurement leads, and founders of small-to-mid Georgian businesses (legal, retail, food, clinics, call centers) equipping or refreshing an office. They arrive from a referral or search, often on a phone, and want to know three things fast: what Infra Core supplies, whether it's trustworthy (official warranty, real company), and how to get a quote. Bilingual audience; Georgian is the default, English for international staff.

## Product Purpose

Infra Core is a B2B IT supplier in Georgia: computers, networking, printers, Jabra headsets, software licensing, delivered and installed turnkey with official warranty. The site's job is to generate quote requests (form, phone, Telegram/WhatsApp). Success = a visitor understands the one-supplier-for-the-whole-office promise within seconds and submits an inquiry.

## Brand Personality

Established, procurement-grade, direct. The site should feel like a national IT distributor (reference: noventiq.ge), not a startup landing page: real product photography, a prominent phone number, dense but ordered commerce layout, deep corporate blue, generous white. Confidence comes from structure and specificity, not slogans.

## Anti-references

- Startup-SaaS gradients, glassmorphism, hero metrics, playful illustration.
- Thin gray text-on-white "minimal portfolio" look; this is commerce, density is a feature.
- The 2023 AI-landing scaffold: tracked uppercase eyebrows above every section, identical icon-card grids, centered everything.

## Design Principles

1. **Catalog first.** Product categories are the merchandise; show them as image-led tiles a buyer can scan, not abstract icon cards.
2. **Contact is the conversion.** Phone, email, and "request a quote" visible at every scroll depth; the header carries the phone number like a trade counter.
3. **Distributor blue.** One committed corporate blue (#0B408B family) on white with light-gray section bands; navy for hero/footer. No rainbow accents.
4. **Bilingual without compromise.** Georgian text must look as deliberate as English: shorter line lengths, slightly larger leading, no overflowing headlines.
5. **Faithful claims only.** Copy comes from dictionaries.ts; the design never invents products, prices, or partners.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 body contrast, visible focus states, full keyboard nav (drawer already manages inert/Escape), prefers-reduced-motion alternatives for all reveals, alt text on category imagery in both languages.
